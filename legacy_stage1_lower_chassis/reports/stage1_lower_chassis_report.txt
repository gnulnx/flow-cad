Erb Stage 1 lower chassis CAD report
======================================

Final outer dimensions:
- Center structural box: 200.0 W x 240.0 D x 220.0 H mm
- Assembly bounding box with flush medium inserts: 200.0 W x 240.0 D x 226.0 H mm

Axle center height from bottom: 50.0 mm
Side plate base thickness: 12.0 mm
Reinforced axle boss total thickness: 30.0 mm
Replaceable axle insert thickness: 12.0 mm
Fit-safe cross-part envelope: 140.0 W x 200.0 D mm
Top lid footprint: 200.0 W x 240.0 D mm
Flat equipment shelf footprint: 140.0 W x 200.0 D x 6.0 H mm
Flat equipment shelf assembly levels: Z=76.0 mm, Z=146.0 mm
Equipment shelf support brackets: 8 front/rear M4 pads at Y +/-92 mm

Axle insert clearances:
- tight: 16.3 mm diameter, 12.3 mm flat-to-flat double-D profile
- medium: 16.6 mm diameter, 12.5 mm flat-to-flat double-D profile
- loose: 16.9 mm diameter, 12.8 mm flat-to-flat double-D profile

Screw sizes assumed:
- M5 structural screws: 5.5 mm clearance, 6.1 mm heat-set pilot pockets, 11.0 mm washer/counterbore relief
- M4 service/electronics screws: 4.5 mm clearance
- M3 electronics/IMU screws: 3.4 mm clearance

Assumptions made:
- Coordinate convention: X is robot width, Y is front/rear depth, Z is vertical.
- The 200 mm center_box_outer_width is the structural side-plate outside-to-outside width; axle inserts are recessed flush into the side plates.
- Cross panels and trays use a 140 x 200 mm fit-safe envelope to clear side-plate rails and axle bosses.
- The top lid is now a top cap spanning 200 x 240 mm; its underside locating lip stays inside the fit-safe envelope.
- Top lid screw holes align over side-wall top-rail M4 heat-set pilot pockets.
- Replaceable axle inserts are 12 mm thick to match the side-plate wall thickness and sit flush in the side wall pocket.
- Axle bosses, ribs, and side rails are capped at 30 mm total local side-plate thickness so the support stack is flush with the raised side-wall rails.
- The generic flat equipment shelf is used twice in the assembly at Z 76 mm and Z 146 mm.
- Front and rear panels include 8 total shelf bracket pads: 2 X positions x 2 shelf levels x 2 panels.
- Shelf support holes use M4 clearance at X +/-58 mm and Y +/-92 mm.
- The bottom tray raised side rails are split into front/rear segments to avoid the central axle boss zone.
- The actual motor shaft is modeled as a double-D axle profile using diameter plus flat-to-flat dimensions.
- The full assembly STEP uses the medium axle insert variant by default.
- Battery retention and controller/PC mounts are intentionally not modeled on the flat shelves yet.
- STEP files are CAD prototypes for fit and print planning; threaded metal hardware, heat-set insert part numbers, and wheel-side spacers still need final mechanical selection.
