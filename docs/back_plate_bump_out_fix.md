# Surgical Fix: Back Plate Rails + Replaceable Bump Outs

Only modify the **back plate slide rails** and the **replaceable bump outs**.

Do **not** change the existing side chassis attachment geometry. Do **not** touch the existing chassis dovetail. Do **not** move the overall back plate shape.

## Problem to fix

The vertical slide rails on the back plate currently look like they are not properly part of the main back plate. They appear under-attached / floating, especially near the bottom, and the top connection looks odd.

Fix this by making the slide rails clearly and fully integrated into the back plate body.

## Required rail behavior

* The back plate is the fixed base.
* Each bump out is a separate removable part.
* Each bump out slides **down from the top** onto the back plate rails.
* Each bump out slides **up and off** for removal.
* The bump out should hide the rails when fully seated so it looks like one clean piece.

## Rail geometry

Use simple straight vertical rails only.

Do **not** use a full dovetail.
Do **not** use angled wedge geometry.
Do **not** use a tight friction fit.

Use a simple captured tongue-and-groove / T-slot-like slide:

* Back plate has the fixed vertical receiver rails/channels.
* Bump out has matching straight vertical grooves/tongues.
* Rails prevent the bump out from pulling straight outward.
* Bump out still slides smoothly by hand.

## Rail attachment fix

The rails must be physically connected to the back plate with solid material.

Add continuous or near-continuous webbing/ribs behind/under the rail features so there are no floating rail sections.

The rail should look intentionally molded into the back plate, not like separate strips hovering on top of it.

Important:

* Attach the rails to the back plate along their length where practical.
* Add a proper bottom support/stop so the bump out seats at the final height.
* Add clean top lead-in/chamfers so the bump out starts sliding easily.
* Do not fill the entire bump-out cavity unless necessary, but use enough material to make the rails truly attached and printable.

## Tolerances

Use PETG-friendly sliding clearance:

* 0.35–0.50 mm side clearance.
* 0.30–0.50 mm front/back capture clearance.
* Chamfer the rail entrances.
* Chamfer the lower leading edges of the bump out sliding features.

This is a smooth mechanical slide, not a press-fit.

## Retention

Use one M4 retaining screw per bump out.

The screw only prevents upward sliding. It should not align the bump out or carry the main load.

## Avoid

* Do not change side chassis attachment.
* Do not touch chassis dovetail.
* Do not redesign the whole back plate.
* Do not add four exposed screw blocks.
* Do not leave rails floating or weakly attached.
* Do not use full dovetails for the bump outs.
* Do not rely on screw tension for alignment.
* Do not make tiny fragile tabs.
